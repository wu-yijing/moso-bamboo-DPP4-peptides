1 Research Overview & Established Scope Overview & Scope

A core pathology of type 2 diabetes is the rapid degradation of GLP-1 by dipeptidyl peptidase-IV (DPP-IV / DPP4). Food-derived DPP4 inhibitory peptides can be taken long-term via diet with high safety. This study takes the complete moso-bamboo proteome as the subject and builds a complete purely-computational prioritization pipeline, delivering a testable shortlist of candidates.

Established project boundary (applies throughout): all conclusions are purely computational methodology results, with no support from any wet-lab experiment (in vitro DPP4 inhibition, IC50 assay, Caco-2 transport, peptide synthesis and activity verification, etc.). Every "validation layer" is replaced by computational methods; biological confirmation awaits future (if any) wet-lab work. Related limitations are in §8.

Research value

  • Novel source: Published food-derived DPP4 peptides concentrate on milk, soy, fish-skin collagen, lupin, etc.; bamboo (Poaceae) as a food protein matrix is almost absent from DPP4 peptide research. To our knowledge this is the first systematic computational mining of bamboo protein-derived DPP4 inhibitory peptides.
  • Methodological credibility: The protocol's credibility is anchored by a docking benchmark of 4 known DPP4 inhibitors + 1 negative control, and the physical meaning of Vina's single score is supplemented by two layers — MM-GBSA and static MM.
  • Fully offline reproducible: Local re-implementation of iDPPIV-SCM + RDKit preparation + Vina docking + OpenMM MM-GBSA, with zero external API dependency.

4 Convergence & Divergence of Four Signal Layers Multi-signal Convergence & Divergence

No conclusion can be drawn from any single metric alone; only by jointly examining composition signal, Vina, gas-phase MM and MM-GBSA can the candidates be localized.

Three energetic metrics give different relative orders

Signal (physical meaning)Order (best → worst)Indicates
Vina dG (empirical score, incl. solvation-like terms)APQIP > LPPGP > APPSQAPQIP best on the surface
Gas-phase ΔE_MM (MMFF94s endpoints, no solvent/entropy)APPSQ > LPPGP > APQIPAPQIP highest gas-phase strain
MM-GBSA ΔG_bind (incl. desolvation/solvation)LPPGP ≫ APPSQ > APQIPLPPGP strongest binding free energy
Root cause of the divergence (localized by the contact profile): APQIP ranks first on Vina (−6.807) and on contact count (82) / H-bond count (6), yet ranks last in MM-GBSA (only −1.29). The reason is that its contacts are highly concentrated on the C-terminal Pro5 (66 of 82), and its 6 H-bonds fall mainly on the gating Arg147 and the crystal sugar chain NAG1201 rather than the catalytic core Glu146. That is, "many contacts ≠ high-quality binding" — a large number of contacts come from tail-end hydrophobic stacking and glycosylation bias, which MM-GBSA's net free energy corrects to near-neutral.

Conversely, LPPGP, though it has the fewest contacts (35), precisely strikes the catalytic Glu146 with 4 H-bonds plus a hydrophobic Pro interface, giving the strongest MM-GBSA net ΔG (−11.97); APPSQ ranks second by combining catalytic Glu146 anchoring, the lowest gas-phase strain (−8.40) and the broadest footprint (41 contacts).

8 Methodological Limitations & Honest Disclosure Limitations & Disclosure

All outputs in this report are computational-method products. Honestly disclosing the limitations of each layer is part of this study's credibility.
StageLimitation
iDPPIV-SCM prescreenIndependent-test ACC≈0.771 (literature≈0.797); benchmark has length confounding (a naive length baseline already reaches 0.820). Its score mainly reflects the real residue-composition signal, used for ranking / soft filtering rather than deterministic judgment.
Vina dGStatic relative score; excludes conformational entropy and explicit solvent; absolute values for known inhibitors are on the low side — a known bias. "Better than Diprotin A" refers only to the relative computational score.
MM-GBSAPocket-restricted single-structure approximation (only ~11 residues within 9 Å of the active pocket form the computation domain); long-range interactions outside the pocket and receptor strain are ignored; −TΔS ignored, receptor glycosylation solvation not counted. Used for relative ranking, not absolute affinity.
Phase B static MMSingle-conformation endpoint method, not MD sampling; conformational entropy not covered.
Overall boundaryEntirely purely computational, zero wet-lab: no in vitro DPP4 inhibition, IC50, Caco-2, peptide synthesis or activity verification. Biological confirmation awaits wet-lab work.

9 Conclusion & Future Verification Roadmap Conclusion & Roadmap

This study built a purely-computational prioritization pipeline for moso-bamboo protein-derived DPP4 inhibitory peptides, prioritizing three Pro-rich candidate pentapeptides from 7,988 unique digestion peptides (APQIP / LPPGP / APPSQ, Vina dG −6.81 / −6.56 / −6.51 kcal·mol⁻¹). The docking benchmark (4 known inhibitors + 1 negative control) proves the protocol correctly reproduces the DPP4 active pocket and the known relative activity ordering; MM-GBSA and static MM further support the binding pose and yield a more credible relative ranking LPPGP ≫ APPSQ >> APQIP. As the first computational study of bamboo-derived DPP4 inhibitory peptides, the results provide new candidates for the development of functional foods from plant (grass-family) proteins.

Suggested wet-lab verification roadmap (if pursued in future)

  • ① Activity assay: Solid-phase synthesize LPPGP / APPSQ (and the questionable APQIP) and measure in vitro DPP4 inhibition IC50.
  • ② Absorption & stability: Caco-2 model to assess oral absorption / intestinal stability.
  • ③ Binding confirmation: Co-crystallize DPP4 with the most promising candidate to confirm the binding mode.
  • ④ Pipeline calibration: Retroactively correlate computational scores with measured IC50 to further calibrate iDPPIV / docking / MM-GBSA weights.
